Resources

Listed below are some great resources to find some more YA books!

I hope this page has been helpful for you on your journey of YA books. If you have any more questions, my contact information is listed.

  • Goodreads

      • On this site, it lets you log books you have read, save books you want to read, and enter book giveways! Goodreads is a great resouce for readers of all ages to connect with other readers. Every year, Goodreads hosts a book award of a variety of categories. Users of Goodreads are able to participate with the voting! Goodreads can also be used on devices.

  • StoryGraph

      • If Goodreads wasn't a favorite for tracking books, StoryGraph is also a great place to go. On the site, you answer a quiz about the types of books you enjoy. From there, it personalizes your recommendations. There is also a feature to search by your mood, so all of you mood readers out there, this might be your kind of place. StoryGraph can also be used on moblie devices!

  • Epic Reads

      • This is such a fun site! HarperCollins highlight books within their publishing company with Epic Reads. There so many things to do. There are quizzes based on popular books on their site and author event alerts. There are videos from authors talking about their books, and there are other readers who share their recommendations and feelings!

  • YALSA

      • YALSA is a great place to find young adult books that have been given popular awards. If you wanna know if your favorite book has recieved an awards, this is the place to go. You can also look up by the award list, author, genre, and award year.

  • YA Books Central

      • YA Book Central is an online community of other book lovers! Yoou can join book clubs or discussion groups while adding book reviews. YABC includes more tha just young adult books. They included middle grade and children books. There are also book giveaway too!
